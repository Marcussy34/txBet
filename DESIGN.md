# txBet interface system

txBet is a minimal, premium operations surface for event-triggered trading infrastructure. It combines Linear-like restraint, Bloomberg-like data clarity, Polymarket-like contract structure, and F1-style timing graphics.

- **Identity:** the monochrome Split-Window Gate mark and `txBet` Inter wordmark.
- **Opening composition:** a full-viewport brand prologue places the lockup across one centered silver beam. It carries only one promise and two actions; on wide viewports two quiet timing rails (match clock left, venue reprice right) frame the beam as instrument furniture. Replay and execution disclosures live in the chapter instruments and the footer. Product detail begins below the fold.
- **Palette:** Carbon Zero. Brand surfaces are monochrome; color is semantic only.
- **Typography:** Instrument Serif for major promises, Inter for body/UI, JetBrains Mono for prices, clocks, latency, and state.
- **Geometry:** 6 px controls, 8–10 px major surfaces, 1 px keylines, generous outer whitespace, dense internal ledgers.
- **Imagery:** the Event-to-Edge Route, agent telemetry tiles, the Latency Corridor, the Quote Convergence Window, the four-gate Execution Interlock, and the venue coverage wall (official third-party marks in their own brand colors — the one surface where non-semantic color is allowed — each row carrying published fee and adapter status). No portraits, stadium photography, balls, trophies, or generic AI imagery.
- **Chapter grammar:** the product story deliberately changes composition as it moves from signal to timing to control. Event-to-Edge is an open reaction tape, Quote Convergence is a full-width timing corridor, and Execution Protocol is the only enclosed interlock panel. Shared line weights, square nodes, typography, and semantic color keep the three chapters in one system without repeating one card layout.
- **Motion:** content is visible in server HTML. The opening beam resolves once, then narrows into the system as the user scrolls. After it resolves, a restrained event pulse traverses the beam on a randomized cadence — event in, through the gate, fill out — pausing off-screen and when the tab is hidden. It carries no visible control: each cycle lasts about 2.9 s — the match-clock rail rewinds into the 63:00 second as the packet fires, and the venue-reprice rail sweeps to its T+800 snap as it lands — and reduced motion removes it entirely, leaving both rails at rest. The rails also shear apart and dim under the same scroll scrub that narrows the beam. The Event-to-Edge Route, Quote Convergence Window, and Execution Interlock repeat short, distinct decision sequences while visible; each has its own pause/resume control and pauses off-screen or when the tab is hidden. The interlock refuses on every fourth cycle. Agent telemetry tiles run staggered scan-cursor sweeps that pause off-screen and under reduced motion, and the decision-evidence report exposes four deterministic windows as tabs whose gate checklist re-resolves per window. Other GSAP paths draw once, and editorial blocks use a restrained 18 px settle. There is no scroll hijacking or pinning. Reduced-motion preferences keep the full system static and legible.
- **Accessibility:** micro labels are at least 11 px; every state has a text label; focus rings remain visible; mobile layouts must not overflow.
- **Truthfulness:** the browser uses TxLINE-format replay events, synthetic venue books, and simulated fills. The credentialed TxLINE smoke client is separate and live execution is not included. The console sits behind a Privy identity gate (login methods follow the Privy dashboard); the landing stays public. The venue roster may list non-integrated venues only with an explicit roadmap status; fee figures must be published venue schedules with a visible date, never measurements. Capture is described as two modes — paired bundles and directional event-adjusted repricing — behind one shared cost gate.
